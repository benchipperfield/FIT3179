World Food Consumption

A visualisation of food consumption around the globe.

Vegetables, Meat and GDP

In general, higher GDP per capita countries have higher levels of meat consumption, and tend to have higher levels of vegetable consumption. Countries with extremely low GDP per capita, namely African countries, tend to have low vegetable and meat consumption.

North American, South American and Oceanic countries exhibit a low vegetable and high meat consumption per capita relative to other countries, with most of these countries clustered in the bottom right of the bubble plot. Guyana however does not follow this pattern, displaying high vegetable and low meat consumption.

European and Asian countries have mixed levels of vegetable and meat consumption. A counterintuitive observation is that Hong Kong has extremely high meat and low vegetable consumption whereas mainland China has extremely high vegetable and low meat consumption.

Food Consumption Change Over Time

In 2017, Africa, Europe, North America, Oceania and South America have below world levels of vegetable consumption per capita whereas Asia and Europe have above world levels of vegetable consumption per capita.

From 2014 to 2017, South America increased their vegetable consumption per capita by 10.29 kg, Europe by 7.57 kg, North America by 5.42 kg and Asia by 0.79 kg. Africa and Oceania only exhibited a small change in vegetable consumption per capita from 2014 to 2017.

In 2017, Africa and Asia have below world levels of meat consumption per capita whereas Europe, North America, Oceania and South America have above world levels of meat consumption per capita. Africa consistently has significantly lower meat consumption per capita compared to the other regions, with all regions except for Asia roughly tripling their meat consumption per capita.

From 2014 to 2017, Europe increased meat consumption per capita by 1.29 kg, and Asia by 0.50 kg. Africa, North America and Oceania exhibit little change in meat consumption from 2014 to 2017. Meat consumption decreased in South America by 2.44 kg from 2014 to 2017.

Mapping World Food Consumption

After selecting the alcoholic beverage food type, it can be seen that consumption is highest in Burkina Faso, a small country in Africa. Furthermore, alcoholic beverage consumption is high in North America, Europe and Australia, and low in Asia. Spice consumption per capita is highest in South East Asia and India, with high levels also in Eastern Europe and some parts of Africa.

Sugar & sweetener consumption is highest in North America, Eastern Europe and Russia, and low in Asia and Africa. Stimulants are highest in western regions and countries including Europe, USA, Canada and Australia, and also in parts of South America.

Fish and seafood consumption tends to be higher in coastal countries. It is highest in Asia and parts of Europe. Offal (Organ Meats) consumption is highest in Mongolia, and high in Australia and some parts of Africa.

Regional Diets

In general, the largest food type categories across all regions are cereals – excluding beer, fruits – excluding wine, milk – excluding butter, starchy roots and vegetables, however there is significant differences in the composition of food consumption across each of the regions.

Asia has the largest vegetable consumption as a proportion of their total food consumption at 18.83% and Oceania has the largest meat consumption as a proportion of their total food consumption at 9.64%.

Oceania has by far the largest oil crop consumption as a proportion of their total food consumption at 10.71%, with the next highest proportion being Africa at 1.77%.

Alcoholic beverage consumption consists of only 2.45% of Asia’s total food consumption while this proportion is over 5% for all other regions, and 9.17% in Europe.
